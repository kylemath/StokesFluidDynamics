The Navier-Stokes Equations

The simulation is governed by the Navier-Stokes equations, which describe how fluids move. These are among the most important equations in physics!

Incompressible Navier-Stokes

Understanding Each Term

  • $\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t}$: How velocity changes over time
  • $(\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}$: Advection ‚Äî fluid carries itself
  • $-\nabla p$: Pressure gradient pushes fluid
  • $\nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}$: Viscosity diffuses momentum
  • $\mathbf{f}$: External forces (like your mouse!)
  • $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$: Incompressibility ‚Äî fluid doesn't compress

Simulation Algorithm

The simulation uses Jos Stam's "Stable Fluids" method, which runs these steps each frame:

  1. Add Forces: Apply external forces (mouse input)
  2. Advect: Move velocity and dye with the flow
  3. Diffuse: Spread out velocity (viscosity)
  4. Project: Make velocity divergence-free (incompressible)

The Projection Step

This is where Stokes' theorem comes in! To enforce $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$:

  1. Compute the divergence of the velocity field
  2. Solve a Poisson equation for pressure
  3. Subtract the pressure gradient from velocity

The Poisson solver uses the Divergence Theorem (Gauss's theorem) ‚Äî a close cousin of Stokes'!
