What is a Fluid?

Before we can understand how fluids move and spin, we need to know what makes something a "fluid" in the first place. Spoiler: it's not just about being wet!

The Simple Definition

Fluid (Physics Definition)

A fluid is any substance that continuously deforms (changes shape) when you apply a force to it, no matter how small that force is.

In everyday English, "fluid" might mean "smooth" or "graceful." But in physics, it specifically means: something that flows.

This includes obvious things like water and oil, but also gases like air! Both liquids and gases are fluids because they don't hold their shape‚Äîthey flow to fill their container.

Viscosity measures how much a fluid resists flowing. Think of it as the fluid's "thickness" or "stickiness."

  • High viscosity: Honey, molasses ‚Äî flows slowly

2. Density (œÅ) ‚Äî "Heaviness per Volume"

Density tells you how much mass is packed into a given space. A cup of mercury weighs much more than a cup of water‚Äîit's denser.

Density matters because heavier fluids have more momentum and are harder to push around.

3. Pressure (P) ‚Äî "Push from All Sides"

Pressure is the force per unit area that a fluid exerts. When you dive underwater, you feel pressure from the water pushing on you from all directions.
