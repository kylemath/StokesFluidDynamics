Green's Theorem (2D)

The first major theorem: circulation around a boundary equals the total curl inside. This is Stokes' Theorem in flatland!

The Big Reveal

Green's Theorem connects everything we've learned:

Green's Theorem (Circulation Form)

For a region $R$ with boundary curve $\partial R$ (traversed counterclockwise):

Left side: Circulation around the boundary
Right side: Total curl inside the region

üèä The Swimming Pool Analogy

Imagine a swimming pool with rotating water. If you want to know how much the water pushes you around the edge, you could:

Option 1: Walk around the entire edge and measure the push (circulation).
Option 2: Drop paddle wheels everywhere inside and sum up their rotations (curl integral).

Green's Theorem says these give the same answer!

Why Is This True?

The intuition comes from cancellation:

  1. Divide the region into tiny squares
  2. Each tiny square has its own circulation (from its local curl)
  3. Adjacent squares share edges‚Äîthe flow along shared edges cancels out
  4. Only the outer boundary remains!
üí° The Cancellation Principle

When two squares share an edge, one traverses it left-to-right, the other right-to-left. These contributions cancel, leaving only the exterior boundary.

In the demo, you can see this: we sum up curl over many small cells, and it matches the circulation we compute around the boundary!

The Divergence Form

There's also a version for flux and divergence:

Green's Theorem (Flux/Divergence Form)

Left side: Flux through the boundary
Right side: Total divergence inside

This says: the net outward flow through the boundary equals the total "source strength" inside. Makes sense‚Äîif there are sources inside, fluid has to leave somewhere!

Mathematical Details

Written out fully for $\mathbf{F} = (P, Q)$:

Requirements

  • The region $R$ must be "simply connected" (no holes)
  • The boundary must be traversed counterclockwise
  • $P$ and $Q$ must have continuous partial derivatives

With Holes

If the region has holes, traverse each hole clockwise (so all boundaries keep the region "on the left"). The theorem still works!

Applications

Conservative Fields

If curl is zero everywhere, circulation around any loop is zero. This means the field is conservative‚Äîwork done depends only on endpoints, not path.
