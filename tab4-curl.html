Curl: Rotation & Spin

While divergence measures spreading, curl measures spinning. If you placed a tiny paddle wheel in the flow, which way would it rotate?

The Paddle Wheel Test

Imagine dropping a tiny paddle wheel (like a pinwheel) into a flowing fluid. The curl at that point tells you:

  • Which direction the paddle wheel would spin
  • How fast it would spin
üé° The Paddle Wheel Analogy

Positive curl: Paddle wheel spins counterclockwise (by convention, this is "positive" rotation).

Negative curl: Paddle wheel spins clockwise.

Zero curl: Paddle wheel doesn't spin‚Äîeven if the fluid is moving!

‚ö†Ô∏è Key Insight

A fluid can be moving in curves without having curl! Curl measures local rotation‚Äîwhether the fluid would make a paddle wheel spin at that exact point.

The Mathematical Definition

For a 2D vector field $\mathbf{F} = (F_x, F_y)$, the curl (which points out of the page) has magnitude:

In words: curl is the difference between how $F_y$ changes horizontally and how $F_x$ changes vertically.

Why This Formula?

Think about what makes a paddle wheel spin:

  • If the vertical component ($F_y$) is larger on the right than the left, the right side of the paddle gets pushed up more ‚Üí counterclockwise spin
  • If the horizontal component ($F_x$) is larger above than below, the top of the paddle gets pushed right more ‚Üí clockwise spin

The curl formula captures this asymmetry: $\frac{\partial F_y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_x}{\partial y}$

In 3D: The Full Curl

In three dimensions, curl is a full vector (not just a number). It points in the direction of the rotation axis, with magnitude equal to rotation speed.

In 2D, we only have the z-component (pointing out of the screen), which is what we showed above.

Examples

Pure Rotation: $\mathbf{F} = (-y, x)$

This field circles counterclockwise around the origin. The curl is: $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial(-y)}{\partial y} = 1 - (-1) = 2$

Constant positive curl everywhere‚Äîuniform rotation!

Shear Flow: $\mathbf{F} = (y, 0)$

Fluid moves faster at the top than the bottom (like wind gradient). The curl is: $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = 0 - 1 = -1$

Constant negative curl‚Äîthe shear creates clockwise rotation!

Uniform Flow: $\mathbf{F} = (1, 0)$

Same velocity everywhere. The curl is $0 - 0 = 0$. No rotation!

Curl vs. Divergence

These are complementary operators:

Property Divergence Curl
Measures Spreading/collecting Rotation/spinning
Physical meaning Sources & sinks Vortices & swirls
Output type (2D) Scalar (number) Scalar (number)*

*In 3D, curl is a vector, but in 2D it's effectively a scalar pointing "out of the page."
