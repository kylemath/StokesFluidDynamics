Divergence: Sources & Sinks

Is fluid being created or destroyed at a point? Divergence measures how much a vector field is "spreading out" or "collecting together" at each location.

The Intuition

Imagine you're standing in a flowing river. At your location, is more water arriving than leaving? Or is water spreading away from you?

üöø Think of it This Way

Positive divergence (Source): Like a showerhead‚Äîwater appears from nowhere and spreads outward.

Negative divergence (Sink): Like a drain‚Äîwater disappears into nowhere as it collects inward.

Zero divergence: What flows in equals what flows out‚Äîno creation or destruction.

Breaking It Down

The symbol $\nabla$ (called "nabla" or "del") represents the gradient operator. When we write $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}$, we're taking the "dot product" of the gradient with the field.

The partial derivative $\frac{\partial F_x}{\partial x}$ measures: "If I move a tiny bit in the x-direction, how much does the x-component of velocity change?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Confusion

"Divergence" in math doesn't mean "going different ways" like in everyday English. It specifically means spreading out from a point. Even if vectors point in different directions, if they don't spread or collect, divergence is zero.

Examples

Pure Source: $\mathbf{F} = (x, y)$

At every point, the field points away from the origin. The divergence is $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 1 + 1 = 2$ everywhere‚Äîconstant positive divergence!

Rotation: $\mathbf{F} = (-y, x)$

This field spins around the origin. The divergence is $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 0 + 0 = 0$‚Äîno spreading, just spinning!

üí° Key Insight

Rotational flow has zero divergence. The fluid isn't being created or destroyed‚Äîit's just going in circles. We'll explore this rotation with curl in the next chapter!

Why Divergence Matters

Divergence connects to fundamental physics laws:

  • Conservation of mass: For incompressible fluids (like water), divergence must be zero everywhere‚Äîmass can't appear or disappear!
  • Gauss's Law: In electromagnetism, electric field divergence equals charge density‚Äîcharges are "sources" of electric field.
  • Heat flow: Divergence of heat flow indicates heating or cooling.
