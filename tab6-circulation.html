üö∂ Walking Around a Block

If you walk around a city block and the wind is blowing, circulation measures the net effect: did the wind help you more than it hindered you overall?

Positive circulation: You're pushed counterclockwise around the loop.
Negative circulation: You're pushed clockwise.
Zero circulation: What helps on one side hurts equally on the other.

Circulation and Rotation

Here's the key insight: circulation is intimately connected to curl.

  • If the field is rotating inside the loop (has curl), you'll feel a net push around.
  • If the field has no rotation inside (zero curl everywhere), circulation is zero.

This connection is exactly what Green's Theorem and Stokes' Theorem make precise!

‚ö†Ô∏è Convention Alert

We always traverse closed curves counterclockwise (positive orientation). This matches the convention that counterclockwise rotation = positive curl.

The Stage is Set

We now have all the pieces:

  • Curl measures local rotation at a point
  • Circulation measures total "push around" a loop

The big question: How are they connected?

Green's Theorem (next chapter) tells us: the circulation around a loop equals the total curl inside the region!
